Crop

Cropping an image is like cropping a photograph: you cut off one or more of the image edges, eliminating unwanted parts of the original graphic.

Crop No Loss

Crop No-Loss allows JPEG images to be cropped without any quality loss. Normally every time you re-save a JPEG Image quality is lost due to the nature of the JPEG format.

Crop No-Loss does not always crop on the exact boundary selected due to limitations in the way JPEG images are stored. Typically the crop will be rounded off to the closest 8 pixel boundary.
